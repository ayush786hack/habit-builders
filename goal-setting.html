Why Set Goals?

Goals are like a compass for your life's journey. They provide direction, purpose, and a clear path forward. When you set meaningful goals, you're not just dreaming about the future – you're actively shaping it.

Avoiding Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Don't overcommit on day 1
  • ✅ Start small to build momentum
  • ⚠️ Consistency beats intensity

Actionable Example

Not SMART

"I'll finish my syllabus this week."

SMART Goal

"I'll complete 1 chapter of physics daily at 5 PM."

🧠 Why You Should Set Fewer Goals (At First)

🔹 Step 1: You feel super motivated 🔥

You watched a motivational video or just decided it's time to fix your life. You're full of energy and you want to do everything at once.

So you set 5–6 big goals like:

  • • Study 4 hours daily
  • • Start gym
  • • Read a book
  • • Learn a new skill
  • • Meditate
  • • Sleep early

🔹 Step 2: You follow it on Day 1 ✅

You're excited. You might even complete all your tasks on the first day. You feel proud.

🔹 Step 3: Day 2 feels harder 😓

Now your mind is like:

"Uff… again 6 things? I'm tired."

Maybe you complete some, maybe you skip some.

🔹 Step 4: Day 3 you feel lazy or skip everything 😴

And suddenly, you're not as motivated anymore. You might even feel like a failure. You don't feel good, and now it's easier to quit.

✅ So what's the better way?

🔸 Start with just 1–2 small goals

Pick only a few important things, like:

  • "Study one topic every day"
  • "Do 10 push-ups"
  • "Read 2 pages of a book"

🔸 Why this works:

  • Your mind feels relaxed (because it's not overloaded)
  • You actually complete your task
  • That success makes you happy and motivated
  • Slowly, your confidence increases
  • → You build momentum

🔸 After 5–6 days of success:

Now your brain is used to it. You feel stronger and more focused.

👉 This is the right time to add 1 more task.

🧩 Final Tip:
If you want to build long-lasting habits, don't rush.
Build slowly → Stay consistent → Win big.

SMART Goals Framework

The SMART framework helps you create goals that are clear and achievable. Each letter represents a key characteristic of effective goal setting:

S

Specific

M

Measurable

A

Achievable

R

Relevant

T

Time-bound
